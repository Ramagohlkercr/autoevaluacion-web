SpaceX's stock market blast-off could be Musk's biggest gamble yet

Simon Jack

Business editor

18 hours ago

It's 07:25 am, 13 October 2024, at Starbase, near Boca Chica on the Texas side of the US/Mexico border, and on the launch pad stands the biggest rocket ever made. Its engines fire and it climbs into the skies over the Gulf of Mexico to cheers and screams in the SpaceX control room.

But the launch is not the main event. What goes up must come down – and how it comes down will become a milestone in space exploration.

Seven minutes later, the massive rocket booster that blasted the craft towards space starts falling back to Earth – until its engines reignite as planned. It slows its descent and guides itself with pinpoint precision so it can be captured by a clasp called Mechazilla, or "the chopsticks".

On 12 June, trading will begin in a chunk of shares in a company that, up to now, only Musk and a select group of rich private institutions have been able or invited to own. But SpaceX isn't just about rockets – it's a bet on the future of artificial intelligence (AI).

A staggering valuation

SpaceX has filed for an initial public offering (IPO) of its shares. The bankers selling the shares have put a target price tag on the company of $1.75trn – which puts it comfortably in the top 10 most valuable companies on Earth.

That is a staggering valuation for a company that lost nearly $5bn (£3.7bn) last year. SpaceX is in fact several businesses in one company. It designs rockets as well as manufacturing and launching its own and other people's satellites.

"If we look at the business itself, it's really unclear as to what business or industry SpaceX is even in." Sinead O'Sullivan, economist

Big bet on AI

The real bet is on AI because bundled into SpaceX is Elon Musk's AI company xAI, along with a deeper space programme with plans to create data centres in space. Of the $28.5trn market that SpaceX has identified for its services, $26.5trn of that is in AI.

Ownership without control?

Even though he only owns 42% of the company, his shares come with extra voting rights meaning he effectively controls 85% of the company. Musk is listed as founder, chief executive, chief technical officer and chairman of the board.

Another dot com boom?

The SpaceX IPO is the biggest sale of its type in history but it's just the first of a slew of mega-sales of shares in the companies at the frontier of the AI economy. And so, as in 2024, all eyes are on the SpaceX launch pad for the most significant share sale in stock market history.
